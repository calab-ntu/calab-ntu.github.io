This is an invited review paper in Living Reviews in Computational Astrophysics. We first outline the governing equations and distinctive features of fuzzy dark matter. We then present a range of numerical algorithms for both wave- and fluid-based simulations, discuss their respective advantages and limitations, and highlight representative test problems. To facilitate code comparison, we also provide publicly available initial condition files for both isolated-halo and cosmological simulations.
